# My Career Strategy Prompt Playbook
### Navigating a Full-Time Job Search with Advanced AI Prompting

**Purpose:** A 5-module prompt playbook applying advanced prompting techniques to real career strategy challenges — from skill gap analysis to positioning, decision-making, and future-proofing.

---

## What's Inside

Each module tackles a real challenge I face as a recent MBA + MS MIS graduate entering the job market. Rather than using AI as a search engine, each prompt is designed to simulate a specific thinking style — strategic analysis, structured briefing, persuasive writing, logical decision-making, and trend forecasting.

## Module 1: System Prompting

**Prompt Title:** Career Skills Radar for a Data Analytics Job Search

### Scenario / Use Case

As a recent MBA + MS MIS graduate with a background in data analytics, programmatic advertising, and business operations, I want to map my current skills against what top employers are actively hiring for. The goal is to identify where I am strong, where I have gaps, and what to prioritize over the next 60 days to maximize my competitiveness.

### Prompt

```
You are a senior talent strategist specializing in data and analytics hiring for tech, consulting, and SaaS companies.

Evaluate the following candidate profile against current market demand for Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, and Analytics Consultant roles. Structure your analysis across 4 categories:

- Technical Skills
- Domain Knowledge
- Business & Communication Skills
- Education & Credentials

For each category, provide:
- Skill / Area
- Current Strength (Strong / Developing / Gap)
- Market Demand (High / Medium / Low)
- Priority Action (what to do in the next 60 days)

Candidate Profile:
- Education: MBA + MS in Management Information Systems (STEM), Iowa State University, 2025
- Experience: 3.5 years at Accenture — Data Analyst on Google DV360 (programmatic advertising), Business Operations Analyst on Google Fi (subscriber analytics)
- Tools: SQL, Python, Tableau, Power BI, Looker, Excel, ArcGIS, Pandas, NumPy, GitHub
- Domains: Programmatic advertising analytics, subscriber lifecycle analytics, pricing and market analysis
- Projects: Geospatial visualizations, ad campaign performance dashboards, BI decision-support dashboards
```

### Sample Output

**Career Skills Radar — Swati Kumari**

**1. Technical Skills**

| Skill | Current Strength | Market Demand | Priority Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| SQL | Strong | High | Practice advanced window functions and query optimization on LeetCode/StrataScratch |
| Python (Pandas, NumPy) | Developing | High | Complete one end-to-end analytics project using real dataset; publish on GitHub |
| Tableau / Power BI | Strong | High | Build a portfolio dashboard on a publicly interesting dataset |
| Machine Learning basics | Gap | Medium | Complete a focused ML for Analytics course (e.g., Coursera Google Data Analytics) |
| dbt / data modeling | Gap | Medium | Learn dbt fundamentals — increasingly required in modern analytics stacks |

**2. Domain Knowledge**

| Domain | Current Strength | Market Demand | Priority Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Programmatic Advertising (DV360) | Strong | Medium-High | Target ad-tech companies (The Trade Desk, Google, Meta) where this is a direct advantage |
| Subscriber & Lifecycle Analytics | Strong | High | Highlight churn, ARPU, and activation metrics in resume and interviews |
| Geospatial / Market Analytics | Developing | Medium | Keep as a differentiator; relevant for retail, logistics, and government roles |

**3. Business & Communication Skills**

| Skill | Current Strength | Market Demand | Priority Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stakeholder reporting | Strong | High | Lead with cross-functional impact in all interview stories |
| Translating data to decisions | Strong | High | Prepare 3 STAR stories that show business outcome, not just analysis |
| Process improvement | Developing | Medium | Frame Accenture work in terms of efficiency gains and operational impact |

**4. Education & Credentials**

| Credential | Current Strength | Market Demand | Priority Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| MBA + MS MIS (STEM) | Strong | High | Emphasize STEM OPT eligibility clearly on resume and LinkedIn |
| Graduate Certificate in Business Analytics | Strong | Medium | Include in resume header; useful for roles requiring both business and technical framing |
| No industry certifications yet | Gap | Medium | Pursue one: Google Data Analytics Certificate or Tableau Desktop Specialist |

**Top 3 Priorities for the Next 60 Days:**
1. Build and publish two portfolio projects on GitHub (one SQL-heavy, one Python + visualization)
2. Obtain one industry certification (Tableau Desktop Specialist — fastest ROI given existing skill)
3. Reframe all resume bullets to lead with business outcome, not tool or task

### Reflection

System prompting transformed a vague question ("what should I improve?") into a structured, actionable audit. By defining the AI's role as a talent strategist and specifying the exact output format upfront, I got a response that was both diagnostic and prescriptive — far more useful than a generic career tips article. This format is reusable: I can update the candidate profile each month and re-run it to track progress.

## Module 2: Role + Markdown Prompting

**Prompt Title:** Candidate Investment Brief — Recruiter-Style Memo

### Scenario / Use Case

Recruiters and hiring managers spend less than 10 seconds on an initial resume scan. I want to prepare a structured, VC-style investment brief on myself — the kind of document that a recruiter would use to pitch me to a hiring manager. This forces clarity on my value proposition, differentiators, and target fit.

### Prompt

```
You are a senior technical recruiter at a top staffing firm preparing an internal candidate brief to pitch to a hiring manager at a data-driven tech or consulting company.

Write a structured candidate investment memo for the profile below using the following Markdown structure:

## Executive Summary
## Core Strengths
## Domain Expertise
## Competitive Differentiators
## Target Role Fit
## Potential Concerns & Mitigations
## Recommendation

Tone: Confident, analytical, and concise. Write as if briefing a hiring manager before a screening call.

Candidate: Swati Kumari
- MBA + MS MIS (STEM), Iowa State University, 2025
- 3.5 years at Accenture as Data Analyst and Business Operations Analyst
- Clients: Google DV360 (programmatic advertising) and Google Fi (telecom subscriber analytics)
- Tools: SQL, Python, Tableau, Power BI, Looker, Excel, ArcGIS
- Key work: Campaign performance dashboards, subscriber KPI reporting, churn and ARPU analysis, geospatial market visualizations
- Currently based in Ames, Iowa — open to relocation
- STEM OPT eligible
```

### Sample Output

## Executive Summary
Swati Kumari is a dual-degree analytics professional (MBA + MS MIS, STEM) with 3.5 years of hands-on experience delivering data-driven insights for two of the most analytically sophisticated clients in the industry — Google DV360 and Google Fi. She brings a rare combination of technical depth (SQL, Python, Tableau, Looker) and business fluency, having operated at the intersection of data, product, and operations teams throughout her career.

## Core Strengths
- Advanced proficiency in SQL and BI tooling across multiple platforms (Tableau, Power BI, Looker, Data Studio)
- Demonstrated ability to build and maintain production-grade dashboards and recurring reports for senior stakeholders
- Strong analytical communication skills — translates complex data into decision-ready insights for non-technical audiences
- Experience across both B2B (advertising operations) and B2C (subscriber management) analytics contexts

## Domain Expertise
- **Programmatic Advertising:** Deep familiarity with campaign performance metrics (impressions, CPM, pacing, conversion), bid strategy analysis, and inventory delivery — directly applicable to ad-tech, media, and marketing analytics roles
- **Subscriber & Lifecycle Analytics:** Hands-on experience with ARPU, churn, activation, and support volume metrics — highly relevant for SaaS, telecom, and consumer tech companies
- **Market & Geospatial Analytics:** Built geospatial visualizations for agricultural pricing data — a differentiating skill for roles in retail, logistics, and government analytics

## Competitive Differentiators
1. **Google ecosystem experience** — worked directly on Google products (DV360, Fi) through Accenture; understands data at enterprise scale
2. **Business + technical dual lens** — MBA training ensures she frames analysis in terms of business impact, not just output
3. **STEM OPT eligible** — 3-year work authorization without visa sponsorship burden for most employers
4. **Cross-functional exposure** — partnered with sales, finance, product, and customer support teams; not siloed in a technical role

## Target Role Fit
Strong fit for:
- **Data Analyst / Senior Data Analyst** at tech, media, or SaaS companies
- **Business Intelligence Analyst** at organizations modernizing their reporting infrastructure
- **Analytics Consultant** at Big 4 or boutique consulting firms
- **Marketing / Ad-Tech Analytics** roles at digital media companies

## Potential Concerns & Mitigations
| Concern | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| No direct ML/modeling experience | Strong SQL and Python foundation; easily upskilled — frame as "analytics-first with modeling awareness" |
| Accenture work was client-specific | Reframe as consulting agility — delivered results across two distinct, high-stakes client environments |
| Based in Ames, Iowa | Clearly state openness to relocation and remote work on all applications |

## Recommendation
**Recommend for screening.** Swati presents a well-rounded profile with enterprise credibility, strong tooling, and genuine cross-functional experience. She is best positioned for mid-market to large tech companies and consulting firms where business + data fluency is valued equally. Priority outreach should target ad-tech, SaaS, and analytics consulting verticals.

### Reflection

Role + Markdown prompting forced clarity that generic resume reviews never produce. By simulating the recruiter's perspective, the output surfaced how I actually appear to a hiring manager — including concerns I hadn't fully considered. The structured Markdown format made the memo immediately usable as a prep document before networking calls or interviews. I'll update this monthly as my profile evolves.

## Module 3: Style Prompting

**Prompt Title:** LinkedIn Summary Rewritten for 3 Target Audiences

### Scenario / Use Case

My current LinkedIn summary is functional but not tailored. Different employers value different things — a tech company wants technical depth, a consulting firm wants strategic impact, and a startup wants versatility. I want three versions of my professional summary, each tuned to a different audience, so I can adapt my profile depending on who I'm targeting in a given week.

### Prompt

```
Rewrite the following professional summary in three distinct tones, each targeting a different employer type:

1. Tech Company Tone — data-driven, technically precise, product-aware
2. Consulting Firm Tone — strategic, impact-focused, client-value oriented
3. Startup Tone — versatile, entrepreneurial, outcome-driven and concise

For each version, keep it under 120 words and written in first person. The voice should feel natural, not like a job description.

Original Summary:
Analytics professional with experience supporting business operations, programmatic advertising, and data-driven decision making for large-scale products. Proven ability to translate data into actionable insights using SQL, Python, and BI tools in cross-functional environments. Holds an MBA and MS in Information Systems (STEM).
```

### Sample Output

**Version 1 — Tech Company Tone**

I'm an analytics professional with 3.5 years of experience building data pipelines, dashboards, and campaign performance systems for Google-scale products. At Accenture, I worked directly on Google DV360 and Google Fi — tracking impressions, churn, ARPU, and conversion across millions of users and transactions. I'm fluent in SQL, Python, Tableau, Looker, and Power BI, and I'm most energized when I'm turning messy, high-volume data into decisions that actually move a product forward. Currently completing my MBA + MS MIS (STEM) at Iowa State and actively looking for data analyst or BI roles where technical depth and business context both matter.

---

**Version 2 — Consulting Firm Tone**

I help organizations make better decisions through data. With 3.5 years at Accenture — supporting Google's advertising and telecom operations — I've built the kind of analytical judgment that only comes from working across sales, product, finance, and customer teams simultaneously. My toolkit spans SQL, Python, Tableau, and Looker, but my real value is knowing which question to ask before building anything. I hold an MBA and MS in Information Systems (STEM) from Iowa State University, and I bring both the strategic framing of a business analyst and the technical credibility of a data practitioner. I thrive in client-facing, ambiguous environments where structured thinking creates measurable impact.

---

**Version 3 — Startup Tone**

I'm a data analyst who's worked at the pace of Google and can bring that same rigor to a leaner team. At Accenture, I built dashboards, analyzed subscriber churn, tracked ad campaign ROI, and partnered with cross-functional teams — all at once. I know SQL and Python well enough to get things done without a data engineer holding my hand, and I know enough about business to know when the analysis actually matters. Finishing my MBA + MS MIS at Iowa State this year and looking for a team where I can own the data function, not just support it. If you're building something and need someone who moves fast and communicates clearly — let's talk.

### Reflection

Style prompting is deceptively powerful. The underlying facts are identical across all three versions, but the voice, emphasis, and vocabulary shift completely based on the target audience. This is something most job seekers never do — they write one generic summary and blast it everywhere. Having three pre-written versions means I can update my LinkedIn in under two minutes when I shift my outreach focus. The startup version in particular surfaced language I'd never have used on my own.

## Module 4: Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting

**Prompt Title:** Should I Specialize in Ad-Tech Analytics or Pursue Broad Data Roles?

### Scenario / Use Case

I have a clear fork in my job search: leverage my specific Google DV360 and programmatic advertising experience to target ad-tech companies (narrower market, higher fit), or pursue general data analyst and BI roles across all industries (broader market, more competition). Before committing to a strategy, I want to think through this decision systematically.

### Prompt

```
You are a career strategist advising a recent MBA + MS MIS graduate on her job search approach.

She has 3.5 years of analytics experience — specifically in programmatic advertising (Google DV360) and subscriber analytics (Google Fi). She now faces a strategic choice:

Option A: Specialize — target ad-tech, media, and digital marketing analytics roles where her DV360 experience is a direct advantage
Option B: Go Broad — apply to general Data Analyst and BI Analyst roles across all industries

Think through this decision step by step across the following 5 dimensions:

1. Market Size & Competition
   - How large is each opportunity pool?
   - How competitive is each path?

2. Skill Transferability
   - How well do her current skills map to each path?
   - What gaps exist in each direction?

3. Speed to Offer
   - Which path is likely to yield a faster offer?
   - What factors affect time-to-hire in each case?

4. Long-Term Career Value
   - Which path builds more durable, valuable expertise over 3-5 years?
   - Where does each path lead?

5. Personal Risk Profile
   - What does each path look like if things go slowly?
   - Which is more resilient to market shifts?

After analyzing all five dimensions, provide:
- A clear recommendation (Option A, Option B, or a hybrid)
- A 30-60-90 day action plan based on the recommendation
```

### Sample Output

**Step-by-Step Analysis**

**1. Market Size & Competition**
- Option A (Ad-Tech Specialist): The programmatic advertising analytics market is real but concentrated — major players include The Trade Desk, Google, Meta, Amazon Ads, DoubleVerify, and agencies like Publicis and GroupM. The candidate pool for these roles is smaller and more specialized, meaning less competition from generic analytics candidates. However, the total number of open roles is also lower.
- Option B (Broad Data Analyst): Vastly larger opportunity pool across every industry. However, competition is significantly higher — these roles attract candidates from all backgrounds, and differentiation is harder without a clear domain narrative.

**Advantage: Option A** for quality-of-fit; **Option B** for volume of opportunities.

**2. Skill Transferability**
- Option A: Swati's DV360 experience, campaign KPI tracking, and attribution analysis are direct matches. She speaks the language of impressions, CPM, pacing, and conversion — rare outside ad-tech.
- Option B: SQL, Tableau, and dashboard skills transfer universally. But without domain-specific framing, she competes against candidates with finance, healthcare, or retail analytics backgrounds who have deeper context in those verticals.

**Advantage: Option A** — her differentiation is strongest here.

**3. Speed to Offer**
- Option A: Recruiters in ad-tech actively search for DV360 experience. A well-optimized LinkedIn profile with those keywords is likely to generate inbound interest faster.
- Option B: Higher volume of applications needed; more likely to be filtered out at ATS stage without industry-specific keywords.

**Advantage: Option A** for speed, given targeted outreach.

**4. Long-Term Career Value**
- Option A: Ad-tech is evolving rapidly — cookie deprecation, AI-driven bidding, and privacy regulations are reshaping the industry. Specialists who understand both the data and the business model will be in demand. Leads toward Senior Analyst → Analytics Manager → Data Strategy roles in media or mar-tech.
- Option B: Broader foundation, more role flexibility, but slower to build a distinctive professional identity.

**Advantage: Option A** for distinctiveness; **Option B** for flexibility.

**5. Personal Risk Profile**
- Option A: If ad-tech hiring slows (e.g., economic downturn affecting ad spend), the opportunity pool shrinks.
- Option B: More resilient to sector-specific downturns — data roles exist in every industry.

**Advantage: Option B** for resilience.

---

**Recommendation: Hybrid Approach — Lead with Ad-Tech, Keep the Door Open**

Specialize your outreach and positioning for ad-tech and digital marketing analytics roles first. Your DV360 experience is a rare and specific credential that dramatically increases fit-signal in those applications. Simultaneously, keep a secondary track of general BI and data analyst applications at tech and SaaS companies where your tooling (SQL, Tableau, Python) speaks for itself.

**30-60-90 Day Action Plan**

| Phase | Focus | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1-30 | Build the Ad-Tech Track | Update LinkedIn with DV360, programmatic, and campaign analytics keywords. Identify 20 target companies. Reach out to 5 alumni in ad-tech. Apply to 10 targeted roles. |
| Days 31-60 | Activate the Broad Track | Apply to 15-20 general BI/Data Analyst roles at tech and SaaS companies. Publish one portfolio project on GitHub relevant to marketing or subscriber analytics. |
| Days 61-90 | Assess and Adjust | Review response rates across both tracks. Double down on whichever is generating more traction. If ad-tech is slow, shift energy to broader SaaS/tech roles. |

### Reflection

CoT prompting made a genuinely difficult career decision tractable. Instead of going with gut instinct, I was forced to examine the same choice across five independent lenses — and the answer that emerged (hybrid, lead with specialization) was more nuanced than either extreme I'd been considering. This format is directly applicable to any strategic fork: product decisions, offer comparisons, or role negotiations. The 30-60-90 day output is something I can act on immediately.

## Module 5: Self-Generated Context Learning

**Prompt Title:** Future Skills Radar — What Data Professionals Need in 2025 and Beyond

### Scenario / Use Case

The analytics job market is shifting fast — AI tools, automated reporting, and LLM-powered data analysis are changing what employers expect from data professionals. Before finalizing my skill development plan, I want to understand which skills are appreciating in value, which are being commoditized, and how I should position my learning over the next 12 months.

### Prompt

```
Imagine it is late 2025. AI-assisted analytics has become mainstream — most BI tools have natural language query interfaces, SQL can be auto-generated by copilots, and dashboards are increasingly self-serve for business users.

Step 1: Generate 4 emerging shifts happening in the data and analytics job market as a result of this AI integration.

Step 2: For each shift, describe:
- What is changing
- Which skills are being commoditized
- Which skills are becoming more valuable
- What this means for a junior-to-mid data analyst entering the market

Step 3: Based on these shifts, write a prioritized 12-month skill development roadmap for a data analyst with the following profile:
- Strong in SQL, Tableau, Power BI, Excel
- Developing in Python
- Background in advertising analytics and subscriber data
- Targeting data analyst and BI analyst roles at tech or consulting companies
```

### Sample Output

**Step 1 & 2 — Four Emerging Shifts in Data Analytics**

**Shift 1: Natural Language Replaces Basic SQL for Business Users**
- What is changing: Business stakeholders can now query data in plain English using tools like Databricks AI, Power BI Copilot, and Looker's AI features — reducing dependency on analysts for routine queries.
- Being commoditized: Basic SELECT-WHERE-GROUP BY queries; standard report pulling; manual dashboard refreshes.
- Becoming more valuable: Complex query optimization, data modeling, dbt pipelines, and data quality governance — the work that AI cannot reliably do yet.
- What this means: Entry-level analysts who only write basic SQL will be squeezed. Those who understand the data layer deeply — schemas, relationships, performance — will remain essential.

**Shift 2: Insight Generation Is Automated — Storytelling Is Not**
- What is changing: Tools can now auto-surface anomalies, trends, and variance explanations. The "what happened" is increasingly automatic.
- Being commoditized: Descriptive analytics, standard KPI reporting, trend identification.
- Becoming more valuable: "So what" analysis — translating insights into business decisions, executive communication, and strategic recommendations.
- What this means: The analyst who can walk into a leadership meeting and explain why a metric moved — and what to do about it — is far more valuable than the one who can build the chart showing it.

**Shift 3: The Analytics Stack Is Becoming More Complex, Not Less**
- What is changing: Modern data stacks (dbt, Snowflake, Fivetran, Airflow) are proliferating even as surface tools get simpler. Someone has to maintain the plumbing.
- Being commoditized: Drag-and-drop dashboard building in legacy tools.
- Becoming more valuable: dbt for data transformation, cloud data warehouse fluency (Snowflake, BigQuery), understanding of data pipelines and lineage.
- What this means: Analysts who understand only the visualization layer will be vulnerable. Those who understand how data moves and transforms will be indispensable.

**Shift 4: AI Is a Collaborator, Not a Replacement — But You Must Know How to Direct It**
- What is changing: Analysts are increasingly expected to use AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT Code Interpreter, Julius AI) to accelerate their work. Employers now ask: "How do you use AI in your workflow?"
- Being commoditized: Manual data cleaning, basic scripting, repetitive analysis tasks.
- Becoming more valuable: Prompt engineering for analytics tasks, AI-assisted EDA, ability to validate and critique AI-generated code and insights.
- What this means: The analyst who uses AI to do 10x the work in the same time wins. Being AI-literate is no longer optional.

---

**Step 3 — 12-Month Skill Development Roadmap**

| Quarter | Focus Area | Specific Actions | Target Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Months 1-3) | SQL Depth + dbt Basics | Complete advanced SQL on StrataScratch; start dbt Learn fundamentals | Move from "SQL user" to "data modeler" |
| Q2 (Months 4-6) | Python for Analytics | Build 2 projects: one EDA on ad campaign data, one subscriber churn analysis; publish on GitHub | Demonstrate Python fluency with domain relevance |
| Q3 (Months 7-9) | AI-Assisted Analytics | Integrate ChatGPT Code Interpreter and Copilot into daily workflow; document time savings | Position as "AI-augmented analyst" in interviews |
| Q4 (Months 10-12) | Cloud + Modern Stack | Complete Google BigQuery or Snowflake fundamentals; build an end-to-end pipeline project | Round out modern analytics stack credibility |

**The through-line for all four quarters:** Every project should connect to your existing domains (advertising analytics or subscriber data). Don't learn in a vacuum — build things that your target employers would recognize as directly relevant work.

### Reflection

Self-Generated Context Learning turned a vague anxiety ("AI is changing everything — what should I do?") into a structured, prioritized response. By asking the AI to generate the market shifts first and then use them as context for the roadmap, the output was grounded in a coherent world-view rather than generic career advice. The four shifts it surfaced — especially the point about storytelling and the modern data stack — changed how I think about my development priorities. This technique is now my go-to for any situation where I need to think clearly about an uncertain future.
